BMW i3 is an electric or plug-in hybrid car from the German car manufacturer BMW, produced from 2013 to 2022. The brand's first production electric vehicle, it is part of the BMW i range along with the i8 . The Nissan Leaf is a compact battery-electric car manufactured by Nissan, produced since 2010 across two generations. It was the world's first mass-market electric and zero-emission vehicle. The Leaf has received several awards, including the 2010 Green Car Vision Award and the 2011 European Car of the Year. Its range on a full charge has increased from 117 km to 364 km (EPA rated) through various improvements. Global sales across both generations totaled 577,000 by February 2022 . However, its title as the world's all-time top-selling plug-in electric car was overtaken in early 2020 by the Tesla Model 3.

The Renault Zoe (stylized as ZOE and pronounced as "Zoey"), known as Renault Zoe E-Tech Electric since 2021, is a five-door supermini electric car produced by the French manufacturer Renault. Renault originally unveiled, under the Zoe name, a number of different concept cars. Initially in 2005 as the Zoe City Car and later as the Zoe Z.E. electric concept was shown in two different versions in 2009 and 2010 under the Renault Z.E. name. A production ready version of the Zoe was shown at the 2012 Geneva Motor Show.

The BYD Tang, part of BYD Auto's "Dynasty" series, is a crossover SUV available as an all-electric vehicle, plug-in hybrid, or conventional ICE vehicle. Its first generation, introduced at the 2014 Beijing Auto Show, was initially available as a plug-in hybrid and became a top-selling plug-in electric passenger car in China. The second-generation BYD Tang, debuted in 2018, includes the all-electric variant, known as the BYD Tang 600 and 600D, with a battery capacity of 82.8 kWh. This variant was introduced in Europe specifically in Norway, in 2021.
